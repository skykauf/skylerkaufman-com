How ELO Is Calculated

We use an Elo-style rating that updates after every match. Each player starts at 1500, and your rating goes up when you beat expectations and down when you underperform.

  • Each beach team's pre-match strength is the average of its two players' ratings.
  • Expected win chance comes from the rating gap between the two teams.
  • After the result, the team rating shift is split evenly across both teammates.
  • Base K-factor is 32. In round-weighted Elo, K is multiplied by a round weight.

1) Team rating before match

TeamElo1 = (Elo1A + Elo1B) / 2

TeamElo2 = (Elo2A + Elo2B) / 2

2) Expected score (win probability)

E1 = 1 / (1 + 10(TeamElo2 - TeamElo1) / 400)

3) Team rating update

ΔTeam1 = K × (S1 - E1)

S1 = 1 if Team 1 wins, else 0

4) Player update (split evenly between teammates)

NewElo1A = OldElo1A + 0.5 × ΔTeam1

NewElo1B = OldElo1B + 0.5 × ΔTeam1

5) Round-weighted Elo variant

ΔTeam1 = K × Weight × (S1 - E1)

Weights: Final 2.0, Semi 1.5, Quarter 1.25, Round of 16 1.1, Pool 0.75, Default 1.0

How Matchup Prediction Works

The Matchup Predictor loads each player’s match history, derives a recent-form signal, combines it with career Elo, then estimates win probability and a distribution over final scores.

  • From recent matches: win rate, average set differential per match, and Elo trend across the window.
  • Form points per player combine those signals (same weighting as the live tool):

formPoints = 110 × (winRate − 0.5) + 38 × avgSetDiff + 0.2 × eloTrend

  • Team career Elo = average of the two players’ latest Elo values.
  • Team form points = average of the two players’ form points.
  • Team rating used for the match = career Elo + form points.

Match win probability (Team A)

P(A) = 1 / (1 + 10(ratingB − ratingA) / 400)

Score lines. The model maps overall match win probability to an implied per-set win rate under a best-of-three structure, then samples set scores using calibration tables from the database (matchup_calibration): empirical distributions of loser points for 21- and 15-point sets, split into regular wins and overtime paths. Those set outcomes are combined into 2-0, 2-1, 1-2, and 0-2 bundles; closeness also nudges how tight or lopsided individual sets look.
